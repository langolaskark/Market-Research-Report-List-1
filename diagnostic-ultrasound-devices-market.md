<p><p>Diagnostic ultrasound devices are categorized into various types based on imaging capabilities. 2D ultrasound provides two-dimensional images, commonly used in prenatal exams and organ assessments. 3D ultrasound enhances this by creating three-dimensional images, allowing for better visualization of structures. 4D ultrasound adds real-time movement to 3D imaging, often employed in obstetrics to capture fetal development. Doppler ultrasound focuses on blood flow and can assess vascular conditions, making it essential for cardiovascular diagnostics and monitoring.</p></p>
